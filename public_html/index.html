Agora Technologies

Agora was born after recognizing a need for entrepreneurial resources at the high school level. The lack of such resources is very problematic as it discourages students from exploring entrepreneurship and gaining the confidence that they need to have an edge in today’s competitive job landscape.

As a solution to this global problem, we have developed a year long curriculum to be deployed in high schools that would allow students to explore entrepreneurship by taking an idea from conception to completion using the lean startup methodology while utilizing the business model canvas. The students’ work will cumulate in a regional business plan competition at the end of the term.

Agora was one of the winners of the Fall 2015 Change the World Challenge and is now piloting at the Emma Willard School. We hope to bring our program to other schools in the Capital District and New York City areas in late 2016.

Flo

People spend 37 billion hours a year waiting on lines and that’s just in America alone; individuals can spend up to 5 years of their lives just waiting on lines. The average wait time for grocery stores in Washington D.C. is 8 minutes and 23 seconds. Many stores, especially large ones are inefficient because they lack actionable intelligence regarding customers and their shopping patterns. In turn, customers have a negative shopping experience and don’t become repeat customers.

We built a dual purpose business intelligence solution. Using proprietary facial detection software running on security camera footage in stores, we are able to provide consumers with real time information about wait times and open cash registers, improving the shopping experience. Using the same information and data visualization, we provide business owners with actionable intelligence about customer shopping patterns that allow them to optimize their resources.
